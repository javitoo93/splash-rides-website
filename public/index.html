Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know

The quick answers most people ask before their first booking.

Do I need a boating license to rent a boat?

No. When you book a boat with a USCG-licensed captain on Splash Rides, the captain operates the vessel for you. No boating license is required. Bareboat charters (where you operate the boat yourself) have their own state-specific licensing rules.

How much does it cost to rent a boat?

Boat rental prices on Splash Rides vary by boat type, size, location, and trip duration. Pontoons typically start around $100/hour, center consoles and bowriders $150–$300/hour, and larger yachts $400+/hour. You only pay the listed price plus any captain gratuity — no hidden fees.

When am I charged for my boat rental?

Your card is authorized at booking but only charged 24 hours before your trip. If you cancel more than 24 hours before departure, you get a full refund.

How do I list my boat on Splash Pro?

Download the Splash Pro app, sign up as a boat owner, and add your boat in about 10 minutes: 4–6 photos, basic details (length, year, capacity), your hourly rate, and availability. Our team reviews each listing within 24 hours.

How much can I earn renting out my boat?

A 28-foot boat on Splash Pro typically earns $4,400+ per month at 16 trips/month (a weekend schedule). Earnings depend on boat size, location, and how often you make it available. Splash Pro takes a 20% commission; the rest is yours.

Are all boats and captains insured?

Yes. Every boat on Splash Rides must carry commercial marine liability insurance that meets its state's requirements. Captains are verified with a current USCG license and government ID before they can accept any booking.

Where can I rent a boat with Splash Rides?

Splash Rides operates nationwide in the United States, with active boats in Florida, California, Texas, the Great Lakes, the Northeast, and most coastal and lake regions. Open the app to see available boats near you.

What if I need to cancel my boat rental?

Cancel free up to 24 hours before your trip for a full refund. Within 24 hours of departure, cancellation policies depend on the boat owner's listed terms (moderate or strict). If the captain cancels for safety or weather, you always get a full refund.
